Electric Eel Interactive Display

September 2021 - March 2022 Course: DSGN 384-Interdisciplinary Product Design Projects Software: Figma Developed Skill(s):Soldering, Circuit work, Material Properties, and FMEA
Project Description
  • Problem: Many visitors have a preconcieved idea that there will be lightening bolts coming out of the eel. When they do not see that, they leave the exhibit without reading anything about the eel.
  • Solution: The design creates a more interactive experience by having people connect their real-life to the change in voltage and seeing the changes through lights
  • Users: Visitors at Chicago Shedd Aquarium

Testing & Prototyping

Throughout the process, the main template for the design was constantly changing. Originally, the idea was to just increase visitor engagement with the exhibit. Then the client brought the idea to make the focus on eel advancement. However, with the complexity of the ethics of testing, that idea had to be discarded. After refocusing the design, the initial idea was to have a physical meter and button for the visitor engagement. But after learning the confusion the design had in testing, there was a shift to a digital model which had a higher positive user response.

Why the Design?

The interactive tablet design allows for the visitors to be attracted to the exhibit since there is more to do than just look at the eel. This interactive design is placed in a location that all visitors can use. With the design being on a tablet, integrating more accessiblity modes, gives all visitors the chance to learn about the eel. Most importantly, the display of the voltage in relation to everyday items allows for the visitors to grasp the voltage amount.

The light display will be the main thing to attract visitors to the exhbit because it can be seen from afar. Additionally, it will connect the visitors to the eel as the see the light change brightness and frequency as the eel gets near the visible probes.
