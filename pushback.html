PushBack

April 2019 - June 2019 Course: Design Thinking and Communication 1 Software: CAD Developed Skill(s): Material Analysis and Sketching
Project Description
  • Problem: Patients with pusher syndrome meaning they lean, or push about 10 degrees to either side and perceive this as upright.
  • Solution: The PushBack helps users actively correct their pushing allowing an easier rehabilitation process for both the physical therapist and themselves.
  • Users: Shirely Ryan Ability Lab patients with pusher syndrome over the age of 35

Testing & Prototyping

After researching and learning more about pusher syndrome, my team decided to go down the path of using a brace or strap like design. We had three different brace ideas that varied in rigidness and shape. All the prototypes were tested on the patients at Shirely Ryan Ability Lab on their physical therapy days. With those results along with personal user testing, we were able to see what the best combination to go into the final development stage.

Why the Design?

The final design is a combination of the different braces and included a shoulder strap. The body of the design has foam on the inside to create some flexibility for the user and comfortabliltiy. While the hard shell exterior allows for there to be pressure applied as the user leans over to that side. The slight uncomfortabliltiy and hinderance in movement gives the user signals to stay upright. Additionally, the shoulder strap adds extra tactile feedback and creates a tension force that is slightly uncomfortable. All these signals and feedback allows the physical therapist to focus more on the feet movement rather than their overall posture.
