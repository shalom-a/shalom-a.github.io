NU Tutor App

January 2020 - March 2020 Course: Intro to Human Computer Interactions Software: HTML/CSS/JavaScript Developed Skill(s): Paper Prototyping
Project Description
  • Problem: Students have busy schedules that make it hard for them to attend the free tutor sessions provided or find extra help
  • Solution: The NU Tutor App gave both users the ability to get in contact with the other party and set up meetings smoothly
  • Users: Northwestern University Students

Testing & Prototyping

For the initial prototype everything was done via paper prototype. The paper prototype helped figure out the main features and general structure the users prefered. With that information, the design was then built on HTML/CSS/Javascript. There was 4 rounds of prototyping for the website. Initially, the prototype was getting a sense of interacting with the features from the users point of view. After that, the design started to move into a few features that were not explicitly mentioned but ended up being helpful to the users. For most of the testing there was a focus more on those looking for a tutor than the tutor aspect of the design. This was important in order to scope the project for the timeframe.

Why the Design?

The design is created for both thoses looking for tutors and thoses who are tutors. The design allows for students to chat with one another easily without having to use their personal phone number. Additionally, they can easily filter through the tutors by searching up what classes they are looking for help in or a specific tutorâ€™s name. The students looking for tutors appreciated being able to see the reviews for the tutors and their overall rating. Overall, the design is easy to use and creates the quick connection that both users want.
